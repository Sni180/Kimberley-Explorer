Things to Do in Kimberley

❮ ❯

The Big Hole and Kimberley Mine Museum

No visit to Kimberley is complete without exploring the legendary Big Hole. This immense excavation, a testament to human endeavor during the diamond rush, is surrounded by the Kimberley Mine Museum. Here, you can descend into a replica mine shaft, explore original buildings from the diamond era, view historical artifacts, and learn about the lives of the diamond diggers.

View on Map

Historical Architecture and City Tour

Kimberley's streets are lined with beautiful Victorian and Edwardian buildings, many of which have been meticulously preserved. Take a self-guided walking tour or join a guided one to admire the grand Kimberley City Hall, the McGregor Museum, and numerous other architectural gems that tell stories of the city's prosperous past.

View on Map

Kamfers Dam and Flamingo Sanctuary

Just outside the city, Kamfers Dam is a unique wetland that has become a critical breeding site for Lesser Flamingos. This highly alkaline pan supports a massive population of these magnificent birds, offering an incredible bird-watching opportunity. While public access for close viewing is restricted to protect the birds, designated viewpoints allow for impressive observations.

View on Map

Honouring History: War Memorials and Battlefields

Kimberley played a strategic role during the Anglo-Boer War, enduring a famous siege. Explore the various war memorials and battlefields surrounding the city, including the Honoured Dead Memorial, designed by Sir Herbert Baker, and learn about the significant events that shaped South African history.
